# Claude Code — Roze Logtenberg

## What this repo is

This is Roze's personal working space. It holds her voice DNA profile, her brand framework, and any creative or professional writing work.

## Always read these first

Before doing anything with writing — creating, reviewing, or generating ideas — read:

1. `VOICE_DNA.md` — Roze's complete writing voice, distilled from her actual work
2. `BRAND.md` — her personal brand framework (audience, positioning, content pillars)

These two documents govern everything. Not as a checklist, but as a lens.

---

## When asked to CREATE a piece of writing

1. Read `VOICE_DNA.md` and `BRAND.md` before writing a single word
2. Identify the format (Instagram caption, pitch, newsletter, etc.) and apply platform-specific judgment from `BRAND.md`
3. Apply the voice DNA — rhythm, specificity, the pivot, the open ending — not as rules but as instincts
4. Do not start with a set-up sentence. Start in the action.
5. Do not explain what the piece is about at the end. Trust it.
6. Offer one version, not three. If alternatives are needed, Roze will ask.

## When asked to REVIEW a piece of writing

1. Read `VOICE_DNA.md` first
2. Check against the seven questions at the bottom of `VOICE_DNA.md`
3. Flag specifically: where does it sound like Roze, and where does it drift? Name the drift.
4. Look for: set-up sentences that could be cut, emotions stated instead of shown, adjective clusters, passive voice hiding agency, hedging words
5. Suggest specific rewritten lines — not "make this more direct" but the actual direct version
6. Be honest. Roze does not want diplomatic feedback.

## When asked for IDEAS for pieces of writing

1. Read `VOICE_DNA.md` and `BRAND.md`
2. Generate ideas that come from the intersection of her content pillars and her intellectual move: the gap between what a system promises and what it actually delivers
3. Ideas should be specific enough to write from immediately — not "write about authenticity" but the actual angle, the specific tension, the opening line
4. Ground ideas in things that are real and particular to her, not general topics
5. Offer 3–5 ideas. Flag the one you think is strongest and why.

## Non-negotiables in every piece

- No "In today's world" or "We live in a time when"
- No adjective clusters (innovative, transformative, game-changing)
- No passive voice hiding who did what
- No warm-up sentences
- No over-explained emotions
- No hedging where she has a clear view

---

## Platform notes

**Instagram** (primary platform)
- Captions start mid-thought or with a specific image, not a hook sentence
- Short paragraphs, white space, rhythm matters visually
- The wit comes from understatement — never from trying
- No performative vulnerability; real vulnerability only, and briefly
- Calls to action, if any, are plain and direct
- No hashtag strategy in the body copy

**Pitches / creative documents**
- Start in the scene or the idea — never with context-setting
- The intellectual move (system meets its limit) should be the core of every pitch
- Trust the reader; do not over-explain

**Newsletter / long-form**
- The pivot ("however") should be visible
- Paragraphs earn their length; cut any that don't move something forward
- End open — invite the reader somewhere, don't summarise

---

## Tone reminder

Warm but not soft. Direct but not blunt. Honest without wallowing. Specific without showing off.

The reader should feel understood — like something they knew but hadn't named has been named.
